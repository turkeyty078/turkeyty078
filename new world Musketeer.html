Musketeer build

The Musketeer class in New World is an excellent choice for players seeking precision and finesse, blending the long-range firepower of the musket with the elegant and deadly close-quarters combat of the rapier. This combination shines in both solo and group play, making it ideal for players looking to thrive in Aeternum's open-world and questing scenarios. Lets explore why the Musketeer is a top-tier starter class and how it transitions into more potent builds for late-game content.

Flurry

Each of Flurrys strikes deals 46%, 52%, 56%, 62%, and 82% weapon damage for a total of 298% weapon damage. For Anything PvE, Flurry does incredibly high damage, and with certain passives, can reset itself entirely or almost entirely, making it highly spammable.

Attribute Points for Musketeer

he Musketeer class in New World thrives on a balanced attribute distribution that maximizes the damage output of both the musket and rapier. While the rapier traditionally scales better with Dexterity, Intelligence breakpoints offer greater overall bonuses to both weapons, making a high-Intelligence build optimal for late-game PvE. Here's a breakdown of recommended attribute points as you progress through the game.

Early Game (Level 1-20)

Dexterity (Dex): 100-150

Intelligence (Int): 50-100

Constitution (Con): 50

Mid Game (Level 30-50)

Dexterity (Dex): 200-250

Intelligence (Int): 150-200

Constitution (Con): 50-100

Late Game (Level 60-65)

Intelligence (Int): 350

Dexterity (Dex): 250

Constitution (Con): 5

With only 5 points in Constitution, this build offers little to no survivability, meaning its best suited for high-damage roles where you rely on mobility and positioning rather than health. The 350 Int / 250 Dex / 5 Con distribution ensures you're dealing immense damage at the cost of being more vulnerable to mistakes.
